# Writing

Applies the [trunk](../PHILOSOPHY.md) to my own writing: essays, docs like this
repo, anything in my voice. Writing produced by agents on my behalf is a
different craft with different rules; see [agent-writing.md](agent-writing.md).

Here the grain inverts. In config, almost every deviation is a snowflake. In
authorial writing, voice is the differentiator, the deviation that is the
point. The craft is spending it well: sound like yourself in what you say and
how you frame it, while staying ruthless about everything that doesn't carry
the voice.

1. **A few images, reused.** Primitives over products, applied to prose. Pick a
   small set of concrete images (the grain, the trunk, rot) and build the
   argument out of them. A new metaphor every paragraph is sprawl. One image
   carried through the whole piece is compression, and it compounds the same
   way a primitive does.
2. **Every sentence earns its place.** Minimal surface. If a sentence only
   restates, decorates, or hedges, it goes. What remains should be hard to
   shorten without losing meaning.
3. **Declare, and own it.** State the belief plainly. Hedging treats a sentence
   like a one-way door, and a sentence is the cheapest thing in the world to
   walk back. Write the strong version, ship it, revise in public.
4. **Cut the machine tells.** Em dashes everywhere, forced triples, parallel
   negations, inflated vocabulary. These read as generated because generators
   overuse them, and a voice is worthless the moment it reads as anyone's.
